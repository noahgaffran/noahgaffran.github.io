Composite cased motors

Lightweight experimental rocketry

I've been long fascinated with the possibilities offered by making custom composite cases for rocket motors. Not only can they reduce the weight of the rocket, they also have some operational and integration benefits, allowing for easy fin attachment, ability to have a coupler built into the case itself, and making it easier to have a smooth continuous outer surface of the rocket.

Project Osprey

This project started with small 29mm test motors, then to an ambitious 29 mm rocket that launched with an estimated 200 G of acceleration, and since moved up to a 54 mm motor. In the future I plan to use this now-proven method on more powerful 54 mm motors and fully optimizing the mass of the rocket for maximum performance.

The best way to learn about this project is to watch the video I made after the flight of Deft Osprey to 9,262 ft and Mach 2.0 at URRG in November 2024.
